\subsection{Genetics (doesn't) Align with our Ontological Understanding of Disease}
Leveraging direct and indirect genetic support scores from our best performing model (Methods), we assessed the ability of genetics to recapitulate our ontological understanding of disease. Our trait sets ranged from 3,004 complex traits ingested from genetic associations in the GWAS catalog, 1,005 curated complex traits meta-analyzed over multiple GWAS [], and 1,523 rare disease gene lists constructed from Mendelian disease gene lists from Orphanet []. We mapped all of these traits to their respective ontological terms in the Experimental Factor Ontology (EFO) [] for common traits and the Orphanet Rare Disease Ontology (ORDO) [] for rare diseases.
